Thrive Health Intelligence Platform

Thrive is a consumer health monitoring app that pairs with wearable devices to deliver continuous biometric tracking, AI-powered insights, and a permissioned data-sharing network (Loops). The platform spans a native mobile app, a personal web portal, and an enterprise portal for wellness operators, gyms, and clinical organisations.

    Enterprise Organisation Types

    The Thrive enterprise model is industry-agnostic. Every enterprise account is assigned an organisation_type drawn from a formal schema-level enum, and a separate dashboard_variant that controls the portal's navigation, labels, and feature surface. These two values together determine permissions, terminology (client vs patient), available modules, and the visual identity of the portal experience.

    How the Two-Layer Model Works
    Layer 1 — organisation_type
    Data & Permission Classification

    Stored on the enterprise_organisations table. Drives the fundamental permission gate — clinical vs fitness/wellness — which determines what data practitioners can see, whether AHPRA accreditation is required, and whether the portal uses "client" or "patient" terminology. It also governs which modules are available by default.

    • Enforced by a database-level CHECK constraint — only defined values accepted
    • Clinical types trigger AHPRA gate on sensitive data (vitals, meds, labs, ECG, conditions)
    • Fitness/wellness types expose wellness data to all staff; no clinical data surfaced
    • Auto-sets client_term_preference to "patient" (clinical) or "client" (fitness)
    Layer 2 — dashboard_variant
    UI, Navigation & Labelling

    Drives getEnterpriseNavGroups() in the portal frontend. Controls which nav sections appear, what they are called, and how the portal presents itself — including special-case wording for Elite Sports (squad, athletes, sessions) or Workforce (employees, risk, fatigue). A separate column to organisation_type.

    • Values: default · clinic · gym · care · network · standalone_clinic · elite_sports · workplace
    • elite_sports exists only here — not a formal org type in the DB schema
    • Workforce portal (/workforce) gated separately by workforce_module_enabled flag
    • Psychosocial module similarly gated by psychosocial_module_enabled

    Fitness Organisation Types Open wellness tier · "Client" terminology

    Fitness organisations operate on the open wellness permission tier — all staff see the full wellness data set (vitals, sleep, activity, nutrition, body composition, workouts, AI insights). No clinical data is surfaced. Practitioners are trainers, coaches, and nutritionists rather than AHPRA-registered clinicians.

    gym_fitness
    Gym & Fitness Centre

    Single-site gym or fitness centre. The foundational fitness deployment. Practitioners are trainers and coaches. Full wellness data open to all staff — vitals, sleep, activity, nutrition, body composition, workouts, AI insights. No clinical data exposed.

    • Dashboard variant: gym
    • Fitness Loop role assignment for trainers and coaches
    • Cohort AI Insights and program creation available
    • Integrations: Mindbody, Clubware, external systems
    franchise_network
    Gym Franchise Network

    Multi-site gym franchise with a franchisor and franchisee hierarchy. The most feature-complete enterprise deployment. Activates full network semantics — My Locations (owned) vs My Network (franchisees), compliance scoring, template push, and franchisee oversight — while keeping franchisee data operationally separate.

    • Network org type — full nav and location management
    • Franchisor sees aggregated metrics; cannot edit franchisee records
    • Compliance scoring engine active
    • Bulk upload, activation codes, white-label branding
    • AI cohort insights available (tier-gated)
    • Franchisee-to-operational in under 4 hours (design target)
    Wellness, Health & Care Organisation Types Open wellness tier · "Patient" terminology (auto-set)

    These organisation types share the open wellness permission tier with fitness — no AHPRA gate applies — but are configured for non-fitness contexts: integrative health, employee wellbeing, and direct care delivery. Each has a distinct portal presentation, data emphasis, and module set that reflects its operating environment and the relationship between the organisation and the people it serves.

    wellness_clinic
    Wellness Centre

    Holistic and integrative wellness centres — naturopathy, health coaching, lifestyle medicine, integrative nutrition. No AHPRA gate applies but the context is health-adjacent rather than performance-focused. Full wellness data visible to all practitioners. Well-suited to check-in trends, wellbeing scores, nutrition, gut health, and psychosocial domains. Uses "patient" terminology by convention.

    • Dashboard variant: clinic (or default)
    • Client term auto-set to "patient"
    • Gut health and wellbeing domains prominent
    • Psychosocial module available (if enabled)
    • Women's health and cycle-synced program types relevant
    • Supplement plan and nutrition program features prominent
    workplace_wellness
    Corporate & Workforce Wellbeing

    Organisations deploying Thrive for employee health and occupational safety. Activates the dedicated Workforce portal (/workforce) with purpose-built scoring engines for fatigue, burnout, physiological stress, and heat strain. Designed for safety-critical industries — logistics, construction, mining, manufacturing, utilities. Aggregate and banded views only; raw clinical data is never exposed to employers. Consent is always per individual employee.

    • Dashboard variant: workplace
    • Requires workforce_module_enabled flag on the organisation
    • Fatigue index with KSS-style safety bands (within range → elevated → plan adjustment → stop duty)
    • Burnout risk composite score with team rollups
    • Stress index (HRV-derived) with manager recommendations
    • Heat stress scoring (WBGT-oriented) — red / amber / green status
    • Psychosocial surveys, pathways, and intervention actions
    • Workforce Loop is the most restrictive sharing type by design
    • Aggregate KPIs only — no individual raw time-series for employers
    care_organisation
    Care Organisation

    Aged care, disability support, NDIS-adjacent, and residential care providers. Operates on the wellness permission tier — no AHPRA gate — but the portal is configured for care recipient oversight rather than fitness or performance goals. Activity, vitals, check-ins, and medication reminders are the primary signals of interest. Family Loop integration is especially relevant, connecting carers and next-of-kin to the resident or client's data with appropriate consent controls.

    • Dashboard variant: care
    • Activity, sleep, vitals, check-ins open to all care staff
    • Medication reminders and adherence visible to care workers
    • Family Loop integration for carers and next-of-kin
    • No fitness or performance framing in the portal
    • Wellbeing and psychosocial check-ins prominent
    • Suitable for NDIS, aged care, and disability support contexts
    Elite Sports & Performance Dashboard variant only — not a formal org_type
    elite_sports
    Elite Sports & High-Performance

    Performance sport organisations — professional clubs, national academies, high-performance institutes. Implemented as a dashboard_variant value rather than a formal organisation_type in the database schema. This means it sits on top of an existing org type (typically gym_fitness or standalone_clinic) and overrides the portal's language, nav, and visual framing.

    • dashboard_variant: elite_sports — no corresponding DB org type enum value
    • Portal uses squad, athletes, sessions, performance terminology throughout
    • Not a network org type — no franchise or multi-location semantics
    • Strong HRV, recovery score, readiness, and activity physiology emphasis
    • VO2 max estimation, HR zone breakdowns, cardiac load, sleep quality
    • Health Fingerprint (resilience and baseline drift) highly relevant
    Implementation note
    Elite Sports is special-cased in getEnterpriseNavGroups() — it is not derived from the same network/standalone logic used by all other org types. It is the only variant where squad wording replaces client/patient terminology entirely.

    Access & Security Internal only
    Who can access
    • Role: super_admin — assigned manually in Supabase by a trusted admin
    • Not self-serviceable — no sign-up or invitation flow
    • MFA required on every login, no exceptions
    • Session timeout after 30 minutes of inactivity
    • IP allowlist enforced — office / VPN addresses only
    How it's protected
    • Separate Next.js app — never shipped with customer-facing portals
    • All API calls go through Supabase RLS with super_admin role check
    • Destructive actions (delete tenant, mass export) require secondary confirmation + reason
    • All actions written to append-only audit table — cannot be suppressed
    • No production DB access directly — all ops via controlled Edge Functions
